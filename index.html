Research Focus

My research sits at the intersection of social psychology, AI, privacy, and information security — asking how human behavior, perception, and values shape our relationship with intelligent systems.

I take an inherently interdisciplinary approach: combining psychological theory, empirical user studies, and technical security research to understand how people navigate privacy in AI-mediated environments. My doctoral work, funded as a Marie Curie Fellow under EU Horizon 2020 (PriMa — Privacy Matters), investigated privacy preferences, threat modeling, and multi-party policy management in homecare and clinical contexts.

I now continue this work at Zhejiang University's Department of Psychology and Behavioral Science, exploring how human-centered design principles can guide the responsible development of AI — and how privacy functions not merely as a technical constraint, but as a deeply social and psychological phenomenon.

Beyond the Lab

Music & Piano

若我不在实验室，那我就在钢琴旁。但由于一些原因，最近都在舞蹈室。

Outside research, I find balance and expression at the keyboard. Piano has been a lifelong companion — demanding the same discipline as rigorous research, yet offering something entirely different: pure feeling, unmediated by data.

Having studied and lived across China, Sweden, Norway, Belgium, and Germany, I bring a genuinely international perspective to both my science and my life — curious about how culture shapes privacy, trust, and technology adoption in ways no dataset can fully capture.

I am also a Marie Curie alumna and a strong advocate for early-career researcher networks, open science, and interdisciplinary dialogue between computer science, law, and psychology.
